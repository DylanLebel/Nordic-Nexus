## 12. Sales Order PDF Parser Fix (Completed)

Fixed 4 bugs in `Extract-PartsFromPdf` that caused it to parse 17 bogus entries instead of the correct 8 parts:

1. **Date lines parsed as part rows**: `1 22-Jun-2026 12-Jun-2026 4.00` matched the part regex. Added date-pattern rejection (`^\d{1,2}-[A-Z]{3}-\d{2,4}$`).
2. **Quantity regex too strict**: `tryParseQuantityLine` required the line to END with the quantity number, but pdftotext `-layout` includes trailing price columns. Removed the trailing `$` anchor.
3. **Description lines with trailing prices rejected as noise**: `isPriceNoiseLine` flagged lines like `LOWER DRAWBAR  6.00EA  251.000` as pure noise. Added check: if a line starts with 3+ alpha chars, it's NOT pure noise. Added `stripTrailingPriceNoise` to extract just the description.
4. **Page headers captured as descriptions**: `Sales Order Acknowledgement  2 of 2` was appended to part descriptions. Changed `skipDescPrefixes` from `StartsWith` to `Contains` check, added `\d+ OF \d+` page-number filter.

Verified against real PDF (`Sales Order Acknowledgment_27131_REV_0.pdf`):
- All 8 parts: correct part numbers, revisions, quantities, and descriptions
- JOB CHARGES line (line 9) correctly excluded
- No spurious date-line entries
